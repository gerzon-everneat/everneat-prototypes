Decision Council Report

Is the "soft bundle" the right way to ship Everneat's bottle + refill?

5-member council · blind peer review · chairman synthesis — June 23, 2026

The Council

🔬The Researcher
Evidence-first
Right architecture, but NOT shippable as drawn — it's "soft bundle + 2 mandatory prerequisite fixes."
  • Hosted-checkout + Shopify-authoritative pricing is real → it kills fixed-SKU & most apps → two real lines is the forced-correct choice.
  • Blocking gaps glossed over: the multi-line addCartLines has no sellingPlanId field (only single-line does); scent/size ride two disagreeing hardcoded title lists, no selectedOptions.
  • Backend ambiguity: pairing is read from Supabase but attributes are written to a Fly.io backend — which owns the pairing is undecided.
Confidence: Medium
Bias: favours code-evidence & latent brittleness over ship-speed.
🛑The Sceptic
Stress-test
Sound to create, but it silently decomposes into two unrelated products the moment anything touches it after the add.
  • "Atomicity" is an illusion: lines resolve independently; addCartLines throws after a partial commit; no two-line thunk, optimistic path only touches lines[0], no userErrors handling → it's a reducer rewrite.
  • Deferring cart-grouping is what causes partial-bundle desync — an orphaned refill stuck on a 15% subscription after the bottle is deleted on a checkout you don't control = churn.
  • Discount stacking (50% wholesale + 15% subscribe on an orphan line) is unmodeled; CMS pairing breaks silently on a refill rename.
Confidence: High
Bias: over-weights failure modes & lifecycle debt vs. time-to-market.
♟️The Strategist
Compounding / long-horizon
Ship it — it's the only option that keeps the refill a subscribable line (the LTV engine) while keeping every richer path reversibly open.
  • Pattern scales (per-bundle cost = authoring a CMS pairing, not eng) — but pairing is commerce truth in an editorial CMS → 3-year debt; make it a typed, migration-ready field destined for a Shopify metafield.
  • Subscription-led refilling is the highest-compounding axis; only the soft bundle keeps the refill an independent subscribable line (fixed-SKU/native bundle sever it).
  • Optionality: soft bundle dominates; deferred grouping is reversible; fixed-SKU/Liquid apps are one-way doors. Write _addedFromBundleId on both lines from day one.
Confidence: High
Bias: favours reversibility/optionality & where data lives over near-term simplicity.
🔧The Operator
Execution reality
Very buildable — two-line add is ~½ day; the real cost is the title→selectedOptions rewrite + an external selling-plan you don't control. Ship one-time, one product, flagged, in ~1 week.
  • useVariantSelector rewrite (~15 consumers) is THE schedule risk — its default-fallback is the wrong-SKU bug; a half-migration leaves silent fallback.
  • Subscribe needs a real selling plan configured on the refill (ops/person dependency) → decouple: ship one-time first, gate Subscribe behind "a plan exists."
  • Prod breaks: title rename misclassifies (warn, don't fallback); CMS coupling must drop the bundle not 500; QA must diff PDP-total vs hosted-checkout-total for DTC one-time / subscribe / B2B on the real store.
Confidence: High
Bias: descopes to the smallest shippable slice; weights uncontrolled dependencies heavily.
💡The Creative
Reframe / lateral
Don't ship a "bundle" — make the refill subscription the hero and the bottle a near-free accessory (razor-and-blades / Brita). Reframe "how to bundle" → "how to make refilling the default behavior."
  • Invert the hierarchy: bottle = near-free "device," refill = recurring hero (same two-line plumbing, different protagonist). The neutral "Set/box" framing actively competes with the subscription you want.
  • Add a zero-engineering "Refill Reorder" path (account / post-purchase one-tap refill on a selling plan) — lower risk, serves "refill forever," ship it before the PDP bundle to prove attach demand.
  • Reuse the shipped customer-achievements feature: "you've refilled 12 bottles → ~3 kg plastic saved" streak loop drives subscription; price the bottle as a deposit/refill-credit.
Confidence: Medium
Bias: reframes the question; under-weights the cost of throwing away the designed surface + "free bottle" COGS.

Chairman's Synthesis

The core tension

The council splits cleanly: two engineering-readiness voices (Researcher, Operator) say the soft bundle is the correct architecture but isn't shippable as drawn; one lifecycle voice (Sceptic) says creating the bundle is easy — owning it after the add is the unsolved problem; and two strategy voices (Strategist, Creative) say the real prize is the refill subscription, not the bundle. The sharpest single clash: is deferring the grouped-cart "cheap reversible debt" (Strategist) or the active cause of orphaned-subscription churn (Sceptic)? With a subscription refill in play, the Sceptic wins that one — an orphaned recurring refill is real harm, not cosmetic.

Top options

Recommended
Soft bundle — hardened & sequenced

Build it, but fix the prerequisites first, ship one-time-only v1 behind a flag, and add Subscribe + grouping as gated follow-ons.

Refill-first reframe

Ship a low-risk "Refill Reorder" path (and lead with the refill/subscription) before the bundle PDP. Best done alongside option 1.

Foundations only (not-now)

If the rewrite is too big to absorb, just do the selectedOptions migration as debt paydown — it unblocks everything and fixes a live bug.

Recommendation

Ship the soft bundle — but not the drawn 5-phase plan. Sequence it:
  1. Foundations first (unblocks everything + fixes a live wrong-SKU bug): add selectedOptions/options to the product query & migrate useVariantSelector to resolve by option (scoped to the bundle's products, warn — don't fall back); extend addCartLines/TCartLinesInput to carry per-line sellingPlanId + attributes; make the pairing a typed, migration-ready field and decide its owning backend.
  2. v1 = one-time-only soft bundle, one product (Surface Cleaner), behind the CMS active flag (default off), writing _addedFromBundleId on both lines, with real userErrors + multi-line cart state. QA PDP-total vs hosted-checkout-total across DTC & B2B on the real store.
  3. In parallel (or first, it's cheap): ship the Creative's Refill Reorder path for existing bottle owners — the lowest-risk way to prove the refill-subscription thesis.
  4. Then enable Subscribe (once the refill's selling plan exists in Shopify) and add the grouped-cart card + an orphan-refill policy — because the subscription is exactly what makes deferred grouping dangerous.

Deal-breakers (hard stops)

  • Do not ship Subscribe until a real selling plan is configured on the refill in Shopify — else it silently adds as one-time. Gate it.
  • Do not ship paired lines without userErrors handling + multi-line optimistic state — the current single-line (lines[0]) machinery will desync.
  • Do not ship the cross-listing selector on the current title-parse default-fallback — it silently adds the wrong SKU. selectedOptions is a prerequisite, not skippable "foundation work."
  • Resolve which backend owns the pairing (Supabase read vs Fly.io write diverge) before authoring pairings; a renamed/unpublished member must drop the bundle, not 500 the page.

Dissenting view

The Creative: don't build the bundle PDP at all yet — it's the wrong artifact. The "Set" box competes with the subscription you actually want; the real money (à la contact-lenses / coffee-pods) is the reorder, and the highest-intent refiller already owns a bottle and should never see a bottle SKU again. Ship Refill Reorder + a subscription-hero PDP, measure attach, and the elaborate cross-listing bundle may prove unnecessary. (Counter: the bundle PDP is also the acquisition surface for new customers who don't yet own a bottle — so it's complementary, not redundant; just not first.)
